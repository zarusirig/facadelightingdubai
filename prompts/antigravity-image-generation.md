## GLOBAL STYLE DIRECTIVE (Apply to EVERY image)

```
You are generating a cohesive image library for a premium facade lighting website based in Dubai, UAE. Every image must feel like it belongs to the same visual universe.

PHOTOGRAPHY STYLE: Architectural photography, dusk/twilight "blue hour" or night, shot on a Hasselblad X2D 100C or Phase One IQ4 medium format camera. Tilt-shift lens for building exteriors. Deep depth of field. Crisp details on building surfaces and lighting fixtures.

COLOR GRADING: Deep navy blacks (#04060c), warm gold highlights (#cfad4e), cool blue shadows. Low-key lighting. Rich contrast. Desaturated mid-tones with selective gold/amber warmth on illuminated surfaces. No oversaturated colors. The palette should feel like a Rolex advertisement meets Architectural Digest.

MOOD: Premium, authoritative, engineered precision. Every image should communicate "this was designed by experts." No generic stock photo feeling. No people in frame unless specifically requested. Clean, uncluttered compositions.

LIGHTING IN THE IMAGE: The facade lighting itself is the hero — show realistic LED wash, grazing, accent, or spotlighting on building surfaces. Light should interact physically with materials (reflecting off glass, washing across stone/concrete, grazing textured surfaces). Beam angles should look accurate, not fantasy.

ASPECT RATIOS:
- Hero images: 16:9 (1920x1080)
- OG/social images: 1200x630
- Article inline images: 4:3 (1600x1200)
- Square thumbnails: 1:1 (800x800)

OUTPUT: WebP format, high quality. Every image must feel Dubai-specific — include recognizable Dubai architectural language (modern glass towers, arabesque patterns, desert-contemporary design, marina waterfronts).

DO NOT: Include text overlays, watermarks, logos, people's faces, unrealistic neon colors, fantasy lighting, stock photo poses, daytime-only shots, or generic cityscapes that could be any city.
```
