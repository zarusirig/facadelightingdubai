Dubai Grade Specification for Facade Lighting: Complete Fixture Standard

"Dubai Grade" is the consolidated specification standard that defines minimum fixture requirements across six performance categories — thermal (Ta max ≥50°C), sealing (IP66/IP67), materials (316L stainless, tempered glass, silicone gaskets), coatings (Qualicoat Class 2 or PVDF), electrical (ECAS-certified, surge protection), and controls (DALI-2) — ensuring that every fixture selected for a Dubai facade project will deliver its rated performance and lifespan in the UAE's extreme climate.

This guide provides the complete "Dubai Grade" specification checklist for MEP consultants, lighting designers, contractors, and developers — a single reference for fixture selection and approval that integrates thermal, dust, UV/salt, and coastal requirements into a unified standard.

What thermal specifications define Dubai Grade?

Dubai Grade thermal specification requires: maximum ambient temperature rating (Ta max) of ≥50°C for north/east-facing applications and ≥55°C for south/west-facing, LED driver derating curve confirming ≥90% output at 50°C ambient, heat sink thermal resistance calculated for 50°C ambient (not 25°C lab conditions), and LED module rated life (L70) tested at ≥85°C junction temperature.

Thermal Parameter Standard European Dubai Grade
Ta max rating 35-40°C ≥50°C (≥55°C west-facing)
Driver output at 50°C 50-70% (derating) ≥90% required
Heat sink design ambient 25°C 50°C
LED L70 test junction temp 55-65°C ≥85°C
Thermal cycling test Not required -5°C to +75°C, 100 cycles

What sealing and material standards are required?

Dubai Grade sealing requires IP66 minimum (IP67 for ground-recessed), with material specifications: tempered glass or UV-stabilized polycarbonate lenses, silicone gaskets (not EPDM), 316L stainless steel fixings within 500m of coast (304 minimum inland), marine-grade cable glands with sand-exclusion labyrinth, and PTFE membrane breather valves for thermal equalization.

  • Lens material. Tempered glass is the Dubai Grade standard for premium and commercial applications (15+ year optical clarity). UV-stabilized polycarbonate is acceptable for budget applications with a documented 8-year lens replacement plan.
  • Gasket material. Silicone (VMQ) provides indefinite UV resistance and maintains flexibility across -40°C to +200°C. EPDM is not Dubai Grade because it hardens and cracks under UV exposure within 5-7 years, compromising the IP seal.
  • Hardware material. All exposed fasteners, brackets, and bezels: 316L stainless steel (coastal) or 304 stainless steel (inland, >2km from coast). Zinc-plated steel or aluminum rivets are not Dubai Grade due to galvanic corrosion risk.

What electrical specifications apply?

Dubai Grade electrical specifications require: ECAS/EQM registration (mandatory for import and installation), Type 2 surge protection (minimum In 5kA, Imax 10kA) at each distribution point, Class I or Class II insulation per IEC 60598, DALI-2 or DMX512 control interface, and power factor ≥0.9 at full load.

Electrical Parameter Standard Dubai Grade
Certification CE, UL ECAS/EQM (mandatory) + CE
Surge protection Basic (1kV differential) Type 2 SPD, 10kA minimum
Power factor ≥0.85 ≥0.9
THD (current) ≤20% ≤15%
EMC compliance EN 55015 EN 55015 + ECAS EMC
Control protocol Varies DALI-2 or DMX512 (mandatory)

What coating and finish standards are specified?

Dubai Grade coating standards require super-durable polyester powder coating (Qualicoat Class 2, GSB Master) with minimum 80µm film thickness for painted housings, or marine-grade anodizing (Type III, ≥25µm) for anodized aluminum, or PVDF liquid coating for premium applications — each providing documented UV and salt resistance exceeding 15 years.

  • Powder coating. Qualicoat Class 2 super-durable polyester is the minimum — confirmed by 3,000-hour accelerated weathering test with ≤50% gloss loss. Standard Class 1 powder coat (1,000-hour test) fades, chalks, and loses adhesion within 5-8 years in Dubai.
  • Anodizing. Type III hard anodize minimum 25µm thickness, sealed with hot-water, nickel acetate, or PTFE-modified seal process. Type II decorative anodize (15-20µm) is below Dubai Grade — the thinner oxide layer provides insufficient barrier against salt penetration.
  • Pre-treatment. Chromate or chrome-free conversion coating before powder application is mandatory — it creates the bonding layer between aluminum substrate and powder coating. Without pre-treatment, powder coating adhesion fails within 2-3 years in high-humidity coastal conditions.
