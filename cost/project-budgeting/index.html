Project Budgeting for Facade Lighting in Dubai: 2026 Cost Guide

LED fixture procurement represents 35-50% of total facade lighting project cost — the single largest line item — making fixture selection and pricing the most impactful budget decision. Dubai's facade lighting market spans a wide price range: economy Chinese-manufactured fixtures starting at AED 150 per meter for basic linear washers, through to premium European brands at AED 1,500+ per meter for the same fixture category. The price difference reflects measurable quality differences in luminous efficacy, color consistency, IP sealing durability, and warranty coverage that directly impact the 15-20 year lifecycle cost.

This guide provides current 2026 fixture pricing for the Dubai market, organized by fixture type, with guidance on ECAS-compliant product selection, the real cost of economy vs. premium sourcing decisions, and the hidden costs (import duty, spare parts, warranty terms) that affect the true landed cost of facade lighting fixtures in the UAE.

What do linear wall washer fixtures cost?

Linear LED wall washer fixtures for facade lighting cost AED 150-600 per meter for fixed-white units and AED 350-1,000 per meter for RGBW color-changing units, with prices varying by IP rating (IP65 vs IP67 vs IP68), housing material (aluminum vs stainless steel), beam angle optic, and driver configuration (integral vs remote).

Specification Economy Mid-Range Premium
Fixed white, IP65, 1m AED 150-250 AED 300-500 AED 600-900
Fixed white, IP67, 1m AED 200-350 AED 400-650 AED 750-1,100
RGBW, IP67, 1m AED 350-550 AED 600-900 AED 1,000-1,500
Pixel-mapped RGBW, IP67, 1m AED 500-800 AED 800-1,200 AED 1,200-2,000

The per-meter cost includes the LED module, optic, housing, and end caps but typically excludes the LED driver (remote driver adds AED 80-250 per fixture, depending on output and dimming protocol), mounting brackets (AED 30-80 per bracket set), and interconnection cables (AED 20-50 per meter). A complete installed linear meter of wall washing therefore costs 30-50% more than the bare fixture price.

What do facade spotlights and projectors cost?

Facade spotlight prices range from AED 400-800 for compact accent spots (10-30W) to AED 1,500-4,000 for high-output architectural projectors (80-200W), with RGBW versions commanding a 40-60% premium over fixed-white equivalents.

Fixture Type Wattage Fixed White RGBW
Compact accent spot 10-30W AED 400-800 AED 600-1,200
Medium projector 40-80W AED 800-1,500 AED 1,200-2,200
High-output projector 80-200W AED 1,500-2,500 AED 2,200-4,000
Gobo/pattern projector 40-150W AED 2,000-3,500 AED 3,000-5,000

Interchangeable optic systems (where the beam angle lens can be swapped without replacing the fixture) add AED 50-150 per optic set but provide significant flexibility during commissioning — if the aiming or spread needs adjustment, the optic is changed rather than the entire fixture. Premium brands include a selection of optics with each fixture; economy brands typically supply a single fixed optic.

What do in-ground uplighter fixtures cost?

In-ground recessed uplighters cost AED 300-800 for compact units (5-15W, for pathway and garden accent) and AED 800-2,500 for high-output units (20-60W, for column uplighting and facade grazing), with 316L stainless steel bezels commanding a 30-50% premium over standard 304 grade — a necessary specification for Dubai's coastal and high-humidity environments.

Category 304 SS Bezel 316L SS Bezel Adjustable Tilt
Compact (5-15W, IP67) AED 300-500 AED 450-700 +AED 50-100
Medium (15-30W, IP67) AED 500-900 AED 700-1,200 +AED 80-150
High-output (30-60W, IP68) AED 900-1,500 AED 1,200-2,000 +AED 100-200
Drive-over rated (40t) AED 1,200-1,800 AED 1,600-2,500 Fixed only

In-ground fixtures require a separate drainage sump (AED 50-100) and are installed in a pre-cast concrete housing (AED 100-200) that must be coordinated with landscaping and hardscaping contractors during the construction phase. Retrofit in-ground installation — cutting into existing paving — adds AED 300-600 per fixture in additional civil works cost.

How do economy, mid-range, and premium brands compare?

The three brand tiers differ in five measurable quality parameters: luminous efficacy (economy 90-110 lm/W vs premium 130-160 lm/W), color consistency (economy 5-7 SDCM vs premium 2-3 SDCM), IP sealing longevity (economy 3-5 years vs premium 10-15 years), warranty (economy 2-3 years vs premium 5-7 years), and UAE market support (economy: import-only vs premium: local stock and technical support).

Parameter Economy Mid-Range Premium
Typical origin China direct China OEM, branded Europe (DE, IT, UK)
Efficacy (lm/W) 90-110 110-130 130-160
Color consistency 5-7 SDCM 3-5 SDCM 2-3 SDCM
IP seal durability 3-5 years 5-8 years 10-15 years
Warranty 2-3 years 3-5 years 5-7 years
UAE stock/support Import only, 8-12 weeks Regional distributor UAE office, local stock
ECAS status Often non-compliant Compliant (via distributor) Fully compliant
Lifecycle Cost Reality: A premium fixture at AED 900/m with an 15-year IP seal life and 5-year warranty has a lower lifecycle cost than an economy fixture at AED 250/m that requires gasket replacement at year 4 (AED 150 labor + materials), full replacement at year 7 (AED 250 + AED 200 installation), and produces 25% higher energy consumption due to lower efficacy. The 15-year total cost of ownership often favors mid-range or premium selections for permanent installations.

What hidden costs affect the true fixture price?

Five hidden costs add 25-45% above the ex-works fixture price: UAE customs duty (5% CIF value), VAT (5% on total), shipping and insurance (8-15% of fixture value from Europe, 5-10% from China), ECAS registration and testing (AED 5,000-15,000 per product model), and spare parts inventory (typically 10% of fixture order value).

  1. Customs duty. 5% of CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight) value on all imported lighting products. Paid by the importer and passed to the project. Free zone imports (JAFZA, DAFZA) may defer duty until the product enters the UAE mainland.
  2. VAT. 5% UAE VAT on the total supply value (fixture + duty + margin). Recoverable for VAT-registered businesses but adds to project cash flow requirements.
  3. Shipping. Sea freight from Europe: 8-15% of fixture value (6-8 weeks). Sea freight from China: 5-10% (4-6 weeks). Air freight (for urgent replacements): 20-35% of fixture value (3-5 days). Insurance: 1-2% of shipment value.
  4. ECAS registration. Every lighting product imported into the UAE must be ECAS registered. Registration requires laboratory testing (AED 3,000-8,000 per product), registration fees (AED 2,000-5,000), and annual renewal. Costs are per product model — a project specifying 6 different fixture types requires 6 separate registrations.
  5. Spare parts. Project specifications typically require 10% additional fixtures as spares, held on site for maintenance replacements. This adds 10% to the fixture procurement cost but prevents expensive individual-unit shipments when replacements are needed during the warranty period.
